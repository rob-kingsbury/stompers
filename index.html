The Band

About the Stompers

Born in the humid summers of Louisiana and tempered by endless miles of southern highways, Swamp City Stompers play rock and roll the way it was meant to be played: loud, sweaty, and without apology.

Drawing from the deep well of southern rock, swamp blues, and roots Americana, the band crafts a sound that's equal parts Lynyrd Skynyrd and Howlin' Wolf. Three guitars, a rhythm section that hits like a freight train, and vocals that sound like whiskey and regret.

Since forming in 2019, they've logged over 500 shows across honky-tonks, festivals, and dive bars from Texas to the Carolinas. Their debut album, Mud and Glory, earned praise from Rolling Stone as "the best southern rock record in a decade."

"We don't make music for streaming algorithms. We make music for people drinking beer at 2 AM in a bar that smells like smoke and bad decisions." Jake "Swamp Dog" Morrison, Lead Guitar
